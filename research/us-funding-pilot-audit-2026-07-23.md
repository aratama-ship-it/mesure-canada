# United States funding pilot audit — 2026-07-23

## Decision

MESURE now includes a first United States funding layer alongside the Canadian database. This is a bounded beta, not a claim of complete national coverage. The first layer combines nationwide programs, routes connected to the six U.S. regional arts organizations, and priority state programs in New York and New England.

The database grew from 46 to 63 funding records. Of the 17 U.S. records, 8 are open, rolling or invitation-based on the audit date, 1 has a future announced opening, and 8 are retained as closed-cycle monitoring routes. A closed route is never presented as a current application window.

## Geographic design

- Nationwide: federal, international touring, emergency and jazz touring routes.
- New England: NEFA touring, presenter travel and state routes in Vermont, Maine and Massachusetts.
- Mid-Atlantic: Mid Atlantic Arts and New York State.
- Midwest: Arts Midwest.
- Mid-America: Mid-America Arts Alliance.
- South: South Arts through the national Jazz Road partnership.
- West: Creative West.
- New Hampshire remains searchable through nationwide and NEFA routes. No statewide individual artist grant with a current 2026 application route was verified in this pass, so none was invented.

## Eligibility model changes

The U.S. beta separates four questions that cannot safely be collapsed:

1. State or regional residence.
2. Citizenship or immigration status.
3. Eligible U.S. organizational status.
4. Access to an eligible fiscal sponsor.

Unknown immigration rules remain `Needs confirmation`. The tool only rejects a status when the official source expressly excludes it. Fiscal sponsorship is program-specific: NYSCA Support for Artists requires it, while NEA Grants for Arts Projects and the current USArtists International rules do not allow it.

## Known limits and next expansion

- Exact state-by-state coverage is not complete. States outside the first Northeast group currently use their regional route.
- The Maine 2027 award amount and detailed rules are not yet published; the previous amount is labelled as historical, not current.
- Artistic Innovations has announced an August 2026 opening but has not published the exact current deadline.
- GIG Fund, Jazz Road Tours and Creative West Artist Fund are monitoring records until their next cycles are officially posted.
- A second U.S. pass should add state arts agency programs state by state, beginning with the Northeast corridor and then the Midwest and West, without weakening the primary-source standard.
